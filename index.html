What is AI drug discovery?

The concept, the problem, and why it's happening now.

In one line

Using machine learning and generative AI to find, design, and test new medicines faster and cheaper than traditional trial-and-error lab work.

The concept

The problem
A traditional drug takes ~10–15 years and often over $1–2 billion, and ~90% of candidates fail in clinical trials.
The core idea
Biology produces huge datasets (genomes, proteins, cell images, chemical libraries). AI learns the patterns to predict which molecules will work — replacing much slow physical screening with computation.
The payoff
Higher hit rates and compressed timelines — early data shows 80–90% Phase I success vs the ~52% historical average.
Why now (2024–26)
Three things converged: protein-structure prediction (AlphaFold 3), generative molecule/protein models (RFdiffusion, Chemistry42), and cheap GPU compute at scale (NVIDIA + Lilly, Roche…).

What it includes — the pipeline

Where AI plugs into the six stages of making a drug.

STAGE 1
Target identification
Find the biological "lever" of a disease (usually a protein/gene). AI mines genomic & disease data. e.g. Insilico's PandaOmics.
STAGE 2
Hit discovery
Virtual screening scans huge compound libraries computationally instead of testing millions physically.
STAGE 3
Lead optimization
Refine candidates for potency, safety & drug-like properties. Generative AI designs new molecules. e.g. Chemistry42, RFdiffusion.
STAGE 4
Preclinical (ADMET)
Predict absorption, metabolism & toxicity to kill bad candidates early. FDA now piloting an AI liver-injury (DILI) predictor.
STAGE 5
Clinical trials
AI helps design trials, recruit patients, pick biomarkers & predict outcomes — the 2026 proving ground.
STAGE 6
Manufacturing
Digital twins & AI optimize production and formulation (e.g., Roche/NVIDIA AI factory).

The main technical lanes

Different bets on how AI best finds drugs — and who leads each.

Structure foundation models

Predict 3D protein/molecule structures & how they bind (AlphaFold-style). Leaders: Isomorphic Labs (IsoDDE), Chai (Chai-1), Boltz (open-source).

Biology data / phenomics

Run massive automated cell/lab experiments and learn from the images & readouts. Leaders: Recursion, insitro, Eikon.

Generative chemistry

Design new molecules from scratch to hit a target. Leaders: Insilico Medicine (Chemistry42), Iambic (NeuralPLexer).

Protein design

Build entirely new proteins/binders. Leaders: Xaira (RFdiffusion), Generate:Biomedicines.

Physics + AI simulation

Combine quantum/physics simulation with ML for accuracy. Leader: XtalPi.

Big pharma + compute

Giants buying GPU capacity & partnering with AI-natives. Leaders: Eli Lilly (+NVIDIA), Roche (+NVIDIA), Novartis (+Isomorphic).
